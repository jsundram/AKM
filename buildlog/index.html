How these numbers are made

Local sessions (one timestamped transcript per laptop session): a silent gap that ends in your prompt counts as hands-on — reading, thinking, typing — capped at 5 min per gap. A gap inside Claude's turn counts as Claude working, capped at 10 min per silent stretch, so an abandoned session doesn't bill overnight.

Waiting is credited per continuous Claude stretch on a merged timeline: the first 2 min watched fully, 25% attention after that — alt-tab time, not desk-staring time.

In session is the generous measure — "how long was I actually at this and nothing else": all laptop activity merged onto one timeline, split into working blocks wherever 30 min passes in silence, each block counted wall-to-wall including the passive stretches inside (waiting, reading, testing on the phone). Cloud fire-and-forget time is deliberately excluded — only its prompting minutes count.

Parallel sessions are merged for your metrics (you can't wait twice at once) but summed for Claude's effort; busy is union wall-clock.

Cloud sessions have no local transcript, so they're reconstructed from Claude-Session commit trailers: commits under 45 min apart form a burst, each burst bills its span + 12 min to Claude and 3 min of prompting to you, with no waiting — those were fire-and-forget.

All of it is estimation, not measurement. The knobs live in crunch.py; nudge them and the picture shifts by tens of minutes, not hours.
